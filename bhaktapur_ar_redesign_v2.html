History
Built in 1702 by King Bhupatindra Malla in just seven months, Nyatapola is dedicated to the goddess Siddhi Lakshmi. Each of its five terraced plinths is guarded by a pair of figures — wrestlers, elephants, lions, griffins, and goddesses — each ten times more powerful than the one below.
Key facts
Tallest pagoda in Nepal at 30 m
5 guardian pairs on ascending terraces
Built in under 7 months
UNESCO World Heritage Site component
2015 EARTHQUAKE & RESTORATION
Sustained only superficial plaster cracks. Restoration completed by 2017 — a testament to Newari earthquake-resistant construction.
